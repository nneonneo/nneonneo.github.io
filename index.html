I combine computer science, mathematics, and
electronics to create novel interactive experiences.

I am a third year Ph.D. student in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Chris Harrison and Scott Hudson. I use my background in computer science and mathematics to craft novel interaction technologies, to further improve the computers we use everyday. These technologies range from ways to improve mobile touchscreens, to novel motion tracking systems, and onwards to on-world interfaces. I am a cofounder of the touchscreen technology startup Qeexo.
